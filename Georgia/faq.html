Divorce & Separation

In Georgia, an uncontested divorce typically takes 31-60 days after filing, as there's a mandatory 30-day waiting period. Contested divorces can take 6 months to 2+ years depending on complexity, court schedules, and whether issues like property division or child custody are disputed.

Georgia recognizes both no-fault and fault-based grounds for divorce. No-fault grounds include irretrievably broken marriage. Fault-based grounds include adultery, desertion, cruel treatment, habitual intoxication, conviction of a felony, and mental incapacity at the time of marriage.

Georgia follows equitable distribution, meaning marital property is divided fairly but not necessarily equally. The court considers factors like each spouse's contribution to the marriage, earning capacity, debts, and the circumstances of the divorce. Separate property (owned before marriage or received as gifts/inheritance) typically remains with the original owner.

Child Custody & Support

Georgia courts prioritize the child's best interests, considering factors such as: each parent's ability to provide care, the child's emotional and physical needs, existing parent-child relationships, each parent's mental and physical health, stability of home environments, and the child's preference (if age 14+).

Yes, custody orders can be modified if there's a material change in circumstances affecting the child's best interests. Examples include relocation, changes in work schedules, remarriage, substance abuse issues, or the child's changing needs as they grow older.

Georgia uses an Income Shares Model that considers both parents' gross monthly incomes, number of children, health insurance costs, and childcare expenses. The court may deviate from guidelines based on special circumstances like extraordinary medical expenses or educational needs.

Adoption & Guardianship

Georgia recognizes several types of adoption: agency adoptions, private adoptions, step-parent adoptions, relative adoptions, and adult adoptions. Each has different requirements, procedures, and timeframes. Step-parent adoptions are often the simplest when the biological parent consents.

Timeline varies by adoption type: Step-parent adoptions typically take 2-4 months, relative adoptions 3-6 months, and agency or private adoptions can take 6 months to 2+ years depending on home studies, background checks, and matching processes.
