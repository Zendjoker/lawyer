Know Your Consumer Rights in Georgia

Understanding your rights is the first step to protecting yourself from fraud

Right to Fair Treatment

You have the right to be treated fairly and honestly in all business transactions without deception or fraud.

Right to Accurate Information

Businesses must provide truthful, accurate information about products, services, and terms of sale.

Right to Privacy

Your personal and financial information must be protected from unauthorized use or disclosure.

Right to Seek Remedies

When your rights are violated, you have the right to seek compensation and legal remedies.

Warning Signs of Consumer Fraud

  • High-pressure sales tactics or "limited time" offers
  • Requests for upfront payments or personal information
  • Promises that seem too good to be true
  • Unsolicited contact via phone, email, or door-to-door
  • Refusal to provide written contracts or documentation
  • Demands for immediate decisions without time to consider
