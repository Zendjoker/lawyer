Georgia Child Custody Laws

Understanding how Georgia courts determine child custody

Best Interests Standard: Georgia courts prioritize the child's best interests above all other considerations.

Custody Factors: Courts consider parental fitness, stability, child's wishes (if age-appropriate), and existing relationships.

Legal vs. Physical Custody: Legal custody involves decision-making authority; physical custody determines where the child lives.

Joint Custody Preference: Georgia courts favor arrangements that allow both parents meaningful involvement.

Child Support Guidelines: Support calculated using Georgia's income shares model.
